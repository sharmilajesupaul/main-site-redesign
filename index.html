Interests

What you will find me working on around the clock.

Responsive Design

With various screen sizes that handle a wide range of touch and click gestures, responsive design is more important now than ever before. I aim to build rich applications that remain device agnostic. This means they handle touch and swipe events just as well as dragging and clicking. You will find me testing things on multiple devices and making sure customers experience a seamless integration of features across all devices.

E-Commerce

E-commerce has breathed a new life into small business practices as well as opened up a slew of variety of consumer interactions never thought possible before. Today, your internet presence can have a large impact on revenue. You will find me building features that provide a gateway of communication between consumers and their products. Whether its a 'back in stock' notifier or a simple banner communicating product scarcity, I work towards providing a more robust consumer experience through web application development.

Decoupled Applications

The current trend in web development is moving towards a client that handles almost all storage and data manipulation, this becomes problematic because it becomes increasingly dependent on capability of individual devices. A carefully decoupled application ensures that the server holds majority of the data-manipulation and business logic, ensuring that web applications work as intended on older and newer devices.

Glasshopper

A mobile bar crawling application.

Glasshopper is a mobile bar crawling application built using the MEAN stack (mongodb, expressjs, angularjs, and nodejs), and the ionic framework. This application was a project made at Dev Bootcamp over the course of 10 days. Dev Bootcamp focuses primarily on Ruby on Rails as their application framework and my team decided to veer away from the norm and do a full javascript application. Team members are: Han Kim, Stefano Schiavi, and Neil Lambert
